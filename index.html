Steve Jobs

Remembering an American visionary

The man who co-founded the world's largest technology company

Here's a timeline of his life:

  • 1955 - Born in San Francisco, California
  • 1970 - Meets future Apple co-founder Steve Wozniak through a friend
  • 1972 - Enrolls at Reed College in Portland, Oregon, only to drop out after one semester
  • 1974 - Begins a brief job as an engineer at Atari
  • 1976 - Co-founds Apple Computer with Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne
  • 1977 - Releases the Apple II, the first commercially available personal computer in a plastic case with color graphics
  • 1985 - Resigns as Apple chairman and soon thereafter, starts NeXT Computer
  • 1996 - Returns to Apple, as an adviser, after it buys NeXT for $429 million.
  • 1997 - Becomes CEO of Apple and introduces the Apple Store
  • 1998 - Apple returns to profitability
  • 2001 - Introduces iTunes and the iPod
  • 2005 - Introduces iPod Nano
  • 2006 - Apple's market capitalization surpasses Dell's
  • 2007 - Inducted in the California Hall of Fame
  • 2008 - Introduces the App Store in addition to the MacBook Air
  • 2009 - Takes a 6-month leave of absence for medical reasons
  • 2010 - Introduces the iPad and sells 500,000 during their first week on the market
  • 2011 - Resigns from Apple CEO post and becomes chairman, while Apple starts selling iPhones via Verizon Wireless the same year

"The world rarely sees someone who has had the profound impact Steve has had, the effects of which will be felt for many generations to come."

-- Bill Gates, co-founder of Microsoft Corporation
